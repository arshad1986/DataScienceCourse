Statistical Learning for Stanford University (Material is being transferred here) Website here This is an introductory-level course in supervised learning, with a focus on regression and classification methods. The syllabus includes:
  • linear and polynomial regression,
  • logistic regression and linear discriminant analysis;
  • cross-validation and the bootstrap,
  • model selection and regularization methods (ridge and lasso);
  • nonlinear models,
  • splines and generalized additive models;
  • tree-based methods, random forests and boosting;
  • support-vector machines.
  • principal components
  • clustering (k-means and hierarchical).
